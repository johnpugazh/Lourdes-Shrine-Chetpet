Our Lady of Lourdes Shrine, Chetpet, Celebrates 125 Years of Grace and Devotion

125 Years of Faith, Hope, and Love

1899 - 2024

"Celebrating 125 years of Our Lady's blessings and miracles"

Chetpet, Tamil Nadu â€“ Faith, history, and devotion came together in a grand and unforgettable way as the Our Lady of Lourdes Shrine, Chetpet, celebrated its 125th Jubilee with solemn thanksgiving and joy. The landmark event drew thousands of pilgrims, clergy, and faithful, who gathered to honor the Blessed Mother and to celebrate the shrine's legacy as a center of prayer and spiritual renewal for over a century.

A Historic Milestone of Faith

The Shrine of Our Lady of Lourdes at Chetpet holds a unique place in the history of the Catholic Church in Tamil Nadu. Established in the late 19th century, the shrine has stood as a beacon of hope, healing, and devotion to Mary, the Mother of God. For generations, it has welcomed pilgrims from across the state and beyond, who come seeking comfort, healing, and intercession. The jubilee celebrations were therefore not only a moment of thanksgiving but also a testimony to the enduring faith of the people.

Dignitaries and Spiritual Leaders Grace the Occasion

The jubilee celebrations were honored by the presence of Most Rev. Dr. Leopoldo Girelli, Apostolic Nuncio to India, who served as the Chief Guest of the historic event. His presence brought the blessings of the Holy Father, Pope Francis, to the faithful gathered at Chetpet.

Accompanying him were Most Rev. Dr. George Antonysamy, Archbishop of Madras-Mylapore, and several bishops of Tamil Nadu, who together with priests and religious added solemnity and richness to the occasion. Their collective presence reflected the unity of the Church and the deep reverence for the shrine as a spiritual treasure of the region.

Blessing of the Newly Renovated Shrine

A moment of profound grace was witnessed when the Apostolic Nuncio, together with the Archbishop and bishops, solemnly blessed and rededicated the newly renovated shrine of Our Lady of Lourdes. The shrine, adorned with renewed beauty, stood as a symbol of faith preserved across generations, now shining with fresh radiance for the next century of pilgrims.

As the Nuncio sprinkled holy water and offered prayers of consecration, the faithful knelt in reverence, many moved to tears, recognizing the historical and spiritual significance of the moment.

Messages of Faith and Hope

In his address, Most Rev. Dr. Leopoldo Girelli highlighted the universal role of Mary as the Mother of the Church and a guiding light to all who seek Christ through her intercession. He praised the devotion of the faithful of Chetpet, noting how their unwavering love for Our Lady of Lourdes has made the shrine a place of grace for countless souls.

Archbishop George Antonysamy expressed heartfelt gratitude to the clergy, religious, and lay faithful who worked tirelessly to prepare for the jubilee. He encouraged the faithful to not only celebrate the past but also to continue building a living faith that can inspire future generations.

A Celebration of Culture and Community

Beyond the liturgical celebrations, the jubilee was enriched with cultural programs, devotional music, and Eucharistic processions that reflected the vibrancy and depth of Tamil Catholic traditions. Pilgrims lit candles, offered floral tributes, and joined in prayerful processions, demonstrating their enduring devotion to Our Lady.

The jubilee also served as an opportunity for renewal and mission, calling the faithful to live the values of compassion, service, and unity that Mary inspires.

A Legacy Renewed for the Future

The 125th Jubilee of Our Lady of Lourdes Shrine, Chetpet, stands as a historic and grace-filled moment in the life of the Church in Tamil Nadu. With the newly renovated shrine blessed, the faithful look forward with renewed hope and devotion, carrying the legacy of Lourdes into the future.

The celebrations concluded with the singing of the Lourdes hymn, as the faithful joined in thanksgiving, their voices echoing the prayer that has guided the shrine for over a century: "Ave Maria."
